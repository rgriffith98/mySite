I'm Reece

  I'm a California native and current Undergraduate studying Computer Science with a minor in Entrepreneurship and Management at Johns Hopkins University.

  This summer (2017), I am interning at the Johns Hopkins University Applied Physics Lab as a part of a startup called synoPTis. We are currently working on an Android and iOs mobile application to streamline patient records and data for the Johns Hopkins Medical Center. This will hopefully allow physicians to get a quick "synopsis" on their patients to conserve time and energy in thier high stress environment. I am working as a developer on the front end portion of the project, however due to the nature of startups (for which I am very grateful), I have been able to get some direct experience with our backend using Google Protobuf and Firebase.

  When I'm not working on the synoPTsis app, I work for the Coding School on their codeConnects project as a part-time web developer and instructor. (codeConnects is an online platform that offers one-on-one computer science instruction and mentorship to underrepresented middle and high school students. Pairing each student with a professional software engineer or university student majoring in computer science, students participate in weekly, face-to-face coding lessons using our platforms’ collaborative editing and video chat features.) The Coding School (TCS) is a 501c3 organization that aims to empower the next generation through computer science education. You can find their website here.

  I got my start in computer science by making programs that made high school life easier (ie homework tools, websites, ect.). My experience has led me to be a competetive Computer Science major at Johns Hopkins.

  I'm a maker, a tinkerer, and a hacker. I've traveled to several hackathons and coding competitions and worked on a bunch of projects, from mobile apps to art created with algorithmic programming in JavaScript. I firmly believe in learning by doing and taking education into your own hands.
